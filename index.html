Fiber Optics in the Data Center

An interactive guide to how light moves data inside and between hyperscale data centers — and the companies that build the photonics powering it all.

How does a fiber carry data?

A fiber optic cable is a glass thread — thinner than a human hair — that guides pulses of light down its core through total internal reflection. A laser flashes on and off billions of times per second; a photodetector at the far end turns those flashes back into 1s and 0s.

Core

The inner glass strand (8 µm for single-mode, 50 µm for multimode) where the light actually travels.

Cladding

A surrounding glass layer with a slightly lower refractive index — it bounces stray light back into the core.

Transceiver

A pluggable module (QSFP, OSFP) with a laser + driver on the TX side and a photodiode + TIA on the RX side.

Single-mode vs Multimode

SMF One narrow path for light → long distances (up to 80+ km), used between buildings and for long-reach DC interconnect.

MMF Wider core supports many light paths → cheap VCSELs, used for short intra-rack or intra-row runs (up to ~100 m at 400G).

DWDM — many colors of light on one fiber

Dense Wavelength Division Multiplexing sends dozens of independent data streams down a single fiber pair, each on a different wavelength (λ). A ROADM (Reconfigurable Optical Add-Drop Multiplexer) lets operators add, drop, or redirect individual wavelengths without touching the others.

Click a wavelength to learn what it carries.

C-band

1530–1565 nm. Lowest-loss window in silica fiber. Where most long-haul and 400ZR/800ZR traffic lives. ~96 × 50 GHz channels.

EDFA

Erbium-Doped Fiber Amplifier. Boosts all C-band wavelengths at once — optically — without converting to electrical. Enables 80+ km spans.

ROADM

Uses wavelength-selective switches (WSS) to route individual λ's. Lets operators reconfigure the optical network in software.

The AI squeeze

A single 100,000-GPU cluster burns through roughly 800,000–1,000,000 optical transceivers. Every GPU-to-switch hop is an optical link, and each hop needs two modules (one at each end). That has driven:

  • EML laser shortage — only Coherent, Lumentum, and a few Chinese suppliers can make them at scale.
  • Fiber count explosion — hyperscalers are buying 864-fiber and 3,456-fiber cables from Corning and YOFC.
  • LPO & CPO push — to cut the ~30W/port power of 800G DSP-based optics, Broadcom and NVIDIA are racing toward co-packaged optics.

Who builds the photonics?

The fiber optic supply chain splits into components (lasers, modulators, DSPs), modules (the pluggable transceiver), fiber cable itself, and systems (switches, line systems).
